## 2. BRAND STORY & ORIGIN

It started, as many good things do, in Asheville, North Carolina — a mountain city where the fog rolls in like it has something to say, where artists and wanderers and seekers have always congregated, where the mountains themselves seem to pulse with something older than names.

DMT World put its first designs on Etsy in 2014. That was eleven years ago, and in that time the brand has shipped over ten thousand orders and earned the loyalty of more than three thousand admirers — not customers, admirers — alongside nearly half a million Instagram followers. Those aren't numbers. Those are people who pulled a shirt out of a mailer, held it up, and felt something click.

The brand was never trying to start a clothing company in the conventional sense. It was trying to start a conversation — or rather, to be the shirt that starts the conversation when you wear it to the grocery store and the stranger in the cereal aisle squints at your chest and says, "Wait... is that a machine elf?" And you say, "Yeah." And something opens.

"The goal of this page is to promote a conscious awakening through the use of clothing. These clothes will spark questions from others and create an awareness which will help lead to an awakening." That mission, stated plainly and held without apology, is the gravitational center of everything DMT World makes.

Asheville gave the brand its spirit: mountain-stubborn, arts-forward, counterculturally rooted, unpretentious about depth. Etsy gave it its community: a platform where handmade and heartfelt find their people. And the internet gave it fractal wings.

## 3. BRAND PURPOSE, MISSION, VISION & VALUES

### Purpose
To clothe the awakening — to make visible the invisible truths that hum beneath consensus reality, one wearable spark at a time.

### Mission
Create expressive-art apparel that plants seeds of cosmic curiosity. Every design is a question in fabric form, inviting the wearer and the witness to look inward, look upward, and laugh at the beautiful absurdity of being alive and conscious in this particular universe.

### Vision
A world where more people ask the big questions — What is consciousness? What is self? What is real? — and where that inquiry is joyful, communal, and expressed through art that anyone can wear on their body and their sleeve (literally).

### Values

**Curiosity over certainty.** We don't have all the answers. We make shirts that make you want the questions.

**Art as activation.** Clothing is not passive. A design can interrupt a thought pattern, ignite a conversation, or remind a stranger that they are, in fact, a divine being.

**Cosmic levity.** The universe is, as our founder notes, one big cosmic joke. We hold the profound and the absurd in the same hand, and we laugh — not to deflect, but because laughter is one of the fastest routes to truth.

**Community of seekers.** Our community spans psychonauts, festival-goers, meditators, meme lords, spiritual explorers, and people who just really love fractals. All welcome.

**Integrity of expression.** We make art about consciousness, spirituality, and the psychedelic imagination. We do not instruct, supply, or facilitate. We create; you contemplate.

## 4. POSITIONING STATEMENT & WE ARE / WE ARE NOT

## 5. AUDIENCE & COMMUNITY PERSONAS

### The Psychonaut-Curious
**Who they are:** 22–38, drawn to the intersection of consciousness research, philosophy, and counterculture. They've read Terence McKenna, listened to podcasts about inner exploration, and find the inner-space metaphor genuinely meaningful — whether or not they've had any direct experience. They want to signal membership in a community of thinkers who take the big questions seriously while refusing to be pretentious about it.

**What they need from DMT World:** Authenticity, depth encoded in design, and humor that presupposes intelligence. They'll notice the machine-elf reference. That's the point.

### The Festival Set
**Who they are:** The perennial convergers — Burning Man, Lightning in a Bottle, regional burns, music festivals of all stripes. They live in costume and communal art. They need gear that performs in the field: visually striking, conversation-starting, durable enough to survive a weekend in the desert or a mosh pit.

**What they need from DMT World:** Maximum visual impact. Neon-on-black that photographs from across a crowd. Designs that work as both sincere expression and knowing wink.

### The Spiritually Playful
**Who they are:** They meditate. They talk about chakras unironically. They've done breathwork and perhaps some plant-based ceremonies. They're spiritual but allergic to solemnity. They want their practice to have room for joy and absurdity. They believe that "you are a divine being" is both profoundly true and also kind of hilarious given that they just dropped their burrito.

**What they need from DMT World:** Sacred geometry and cosmic imagery that honors the depth of the tradition, plus enough wit in the wordplay to keep it humble and light.

### The Meme-Native
**Who they are:** Younger (18–28), extremely online, arrived here via Reddit or a particularly good Instagram Reel. They know the McKenna audio clips by heart as cultural touchstones. They send "machine elf" memes the way previous generations sent GIFs. They want apparel that speaks that language fluently and doesn't try too hard.

**What they need from DMT World:** Designs that land like perfect memes — immediately legible, unexpectedly layered, deeply repostable. The wordplay has to be tight. "Otter Body Experience" is for them.

## 7. VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

### Core Voice Qualities
**Cosmic. Witty. Warm. Surprising.**

DMT World's voice carries depth without solemnity, humor without shallowness, and sincerity without earnestness. Every piece of copy should feel like it could have been texted to a friend at 2 a.m. by someone who is both very amused and very awake.

### Tone Variations by Context
- **Product descriptions:** Playful, punny, short. Let the wordplay breathe. Trust the audience to get it.
- **Community posts:** Warm and inviting. "You are all divine beings" isn't a slogan in these moments — it's literally what we mean.
- **Philosophical captions:** One idea, well-landed. McKenna-flavored wonder, minus the lecture.
- **DMs/customer service:** Human, relaxed, delightfully weird when appropriate.

### Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Use wordplay that rewards attention | Use puns just to be clever — make them mean something |
| Speak to the shared cosmic joke | Speak down from spiritual authority |
| Mix the profound and the absurd | Treat one as more legitimate than the other |
| Be warm and inclusive | Be exclusionary or gatekeeping |
| Trust the audience to get it | Over-explain the joke |
| Celebrate the questions | Claim to have the answers |
| Use "you" and "we" — community language | Use distancing corporate "our customers" language |

### Sample Brand Lines
- *"The universe is one big cosmic joke — and you're the punchline and the punch and the comedian simultaneously."*
- *"What if enlightenment looked like a neon elf winking at you from inside your own skull?"*
- *"You are a divine being. This shirt knows it. Now you do too."*
- *"Inner space: the final frontier. Dress accordingly."*
- *"The secret doesn't hide. It just waits for the right question."*

## 9. LOGO & WORDMARK SYSTEM

### The Winking Machine-Elf Mark
**Primary icon:** A stylized entity — half geometric, half organic — composed of interlocking fractal geometry. Its defining feature is one eye in a knowing wink. It is small enough to embroider but complex enough to reward a second look. In full color, the elf renders in Machine-Elf Green with Hyperspace Magenta shadow and Electric Cyan eye-glow. In single-color, it holds in Bone White on Void Black.

### The DMT-Molecule Monogram
**Secondary icon:** The letters D-M-T constructed as expressive-art typography evoking molecular structure — as artistic letterforms, not instructional chemistry. Functions as a standalone monogram for small applications (hang tags, stickers, embroidered chest marks).

### Wordmark
"DMT WORLD" set in Syne Extra-Bold, tracked out at +60, all-caps. The wordmark lives in Bone White on dark and in Void Black on the rare light application. Never stretch, condense, recolor to unapproved palette, or set at less than 24pt.

### Lockups
- **Primary:** Winking Elf Mark + Wordmark horizontal, tagline in Space Mono below
- **Stacked:** Winking Elf above Wordmark (square-format social, patches)
- **Minimal:** Wordmark alone (repeat pattern, small placements)
- **Monogram:** DMT art mark alone (hang tags, small embroidery)

### Clear Space & Misuse
Minimum clear space: equal to the cap-height of the "D" on all sides. Never: rotate the wordmark, apply drop shadows, use on busy backgrounds without a black keyline buffer, substitute typefaces, or render in off-palette colors.

## 11. TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### Display / Headlines: Syne Extra-Bold
Used for product names, campaign headlines, and any text that needs to hit first. Syne carries a geometric quirk — it's confident without being cold, and it holds presence at large sizes in a way that feels like it belongs in both a museum and a mosh pit. Use at 48px+ for hero; 32–48px for section headers.

### Body: Space Grotesk
The workhorse of the system. Warm, slightly unusual letterforms that keep reading pleasant on dark backgrounds. Use for product descriptions, brand copy, extended captions, packaging text. Regular and Medium weights only — let Syne carry the visual weight.

### Mono Accents / Labels: Space Mono
For labels, SKUs, coordinates, technical callouts, and any moment when the copy should feel like it arrived via transmission from another dimension. Use sparingly; it's most powerful at small size as a counterpoint to the expressive display type.

### Scale
- Hero: Syne Extra-Bold, 64–96px
- H1: Syne Extra-Bold, 48px
- H2: Syne Extra-Bold, 32px
- H3: Space Grotesk Medium, 24px
- Body: Space Grotesk Regular, 16–18px
- Labels/Captions: Space Mono Regular, 11–13px

### Pairing Rules
Never use more than two type styles in a single composition. Syne pairs with Space Grotesk for hierarchy; Space Mono replaces captions when a technical/transmission feel is needed. Do not mix Syne and Space Mono at similar sizes — they'll compete.

## 12. IMAGERY & ART DIRECTION

### The Visual World
DMT World's visual language is maximalist by design — but it is *intentional* maximalism, not chaos. The aesthetic draws from:

- **High-saturation fractal geometry** — infinite recursive patterns, Mandelbrot-adjacent forms, kaleidoscopic symmetry
- **Sacred geometry** — Flower of Life, Metatron's Cube, golden spirals; the mathematics that cultures across history have associated with the structure of reality
- **Machine elves and hyperspace entities** — cartoon-adjacent but uncanny; luminous, geometric beings that wink and pulse
- **Hyperspace tunnels** — perspective-collapsing vortexes that imply infinite depth
- **Neon-on-black** — the palette reinforces the subject: bright signal against absolute void

### Photography Direction
When using photography (lifestyle/model shots): dark or natural-light backgrounds that don't compete with the garment. The shirt is the art; the wearer is the vessel. Expression: curious, amused, open — never aggressive, never vacant.

### Art Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Deep, dimensional fractals with clear focal point | Busy noise with no anchor |
| Sacred geometry as structural composition | Geometry as filler |
| Humor in the imagery itself (winking elves, visual puns) | Generic trippy stock aesthetics |
| Reference the inner-space metaphor visually | Depict or imply substance acquisition or consumption |
| High saturation against Void Black | Neon on white or bright backgrounds |
| Consistent palette discipline even in wild compositions | Off-palette colors introduced ad hoc |

## 13. MOTION & HYPERSPACE ANIMATION PRINCIPLES

### Core Motion Language
All animation for DMT World should feel like it's pulling you *in* — deepening, not skimming. The direction is always inward and forward, toward a center that keeps receding.

### Key Motion Concepts

**Tunnel Travel:** Camera movement simulates forward motion through a fractal tunnel. Easing: start slow, accelerate toward center. Never use a loop that feels mechanical — the tunnel should feel like it's breathing.

**Winking Elf:** The machine-elf icon blinks slowly — not cutely, but knowingly. One eye closes over 18 frames at 24fps; holds closed for 6 frames; opens over 12 frames. The closed-eye side emits a brief pulse of Machine-Elf Green before reopening.

**Shimmer:** Highlight colors (Cyan, Gold) animate as slow luminous pulses across design surfaces — not strobing, but breathing. Duration: 3–6 seconds per cycle. Opacity range: 40–100%.

**Sacred Geometry Unfold:** Geometric designs reveal from center outward, each ring appearing in sequence with a subtle glow at the leading edge. Duration: 1.5–3 seconds total.

### Principles
- Motion should feel like revelation, not stimulation. Slow down where you want attention.
- Never strobing, flashing, or high-frequency flicker — both for brand feel and accessibility.
- All animations should loop seamlessly if used as background or ambient content.

## 14. PRODUCT LINE ARCHITECTURE & NAMING CONVENTION

### The Wordplay System
Every DMT World product name is a small act of philosophy disguised as a pun. The naming system works on a double-register: a surface meaning (funny, immediate) and a depth meaning (the actual idea underneath). The gap between the two levels is where the brand lives.

**Formula:** [familiar phrase or cultural reference] + [consciousness/nature/cosmic substitution] = product name

**Examples decoded:**
- **"Otter Body Experience"** — "out-of-body experience" with otter; the cute animal as cosmic vehicle; the gap makes you smile and then think
- **"Fun Guy"** — fungi; the mushroom kingdom as the punchline to a question about consciousness and nature
- **"Youniverse"** — "you" + "universe"; the microcosm is the macrocosm; you're not in the universe, you are one
- **"Terence Creation"** — McKenna + "immaculate conception"; the philosophical lineage as sacred origin
- **"Vote Ayahuasca"** — political parody; deadpan absurdism about what we elect to attend to
- **"Plant Medicine Connoisseur"** — elevation of an earth-based tradition through the most pretentious possible vocabulary; very funny
- **"As Seen on DMT"** — infomercial parody; the inner vision as the only review that matters
- **"Blast Off!"** — the launch metaphor; inner space as the real final frontier

### Product Architecture
**Current line:** Graphic tees and long sleeves, all at $23.
- Positioning: deliberately accessible. The $23 price point is a statement. This is not luxury. This is the truth in a format everyone can afford.
- All products: expressive art apparel. The product is the canvas.

### Future Naming Framework
New designs should pass the double-register test: Does it land on first read? Does it open to something else on second read? If only one of these is yes, revise.

## 16. SOCIAL PLAYBOOK

### Platform Strategy

**Instagram (primary — 471K followers):** The brand's spiritual home. Visual-first; the feed is a gallery. Cadence: 5–7 posts/week, mix of product art, lifestyle photography, philosophical quotes in brand typography, and community reposts.

**TikTok (growth):** Video-first, leaning into motion language — hyperspace animations, unboxing moments, short philosophical provocations (15–30 seconds). Lean into the deadpan delivery of cosmic absurdism; the Cosmic Trickster voice translates extremely well to short-form video.

### Content Pillars

1. **Product Art** — the design as the post; minimal copy; let the fractal speak (30%)
2. **Philosophical Provocations** — one idea, beautifully typeset; McKenna-adjacent wonder without citation overload (25%)
3. **Community** — customer reposts, festival sightings, the shirt in the wild (20%)
4. **Humor/Meme** — wordplay designs featured comedically; the cosmic joke delivered straight (15%)
5. **Brand Story** — Asheville, origin, values, the mission in their own words (10%)

### Caption Formula
Short. One idea. Often a question. Sometimes just the product name and a single line. Trust the image to do most of the work.

*Example:* "Youniverse." / *The whole cosmos, wearing one shirt.*

## 17. BRAND GUARDRAILS & COMPLIANCE

### Expressive-Art Positioning
DMT World is an expressive-art apparel brand. All copy, imagery, and communication speaks to the realms of art, consciousness, philosophy, humor, and spirituality. It does not provide, supply, instruct in, or facilitate access to any controlled substance.

### The Clear Line
- **Always:** Art, imagery, metaphor, philosophy, humor, spirituality, inner exploration as concept
- **Never:** Instructions, sourcing, dosage information, encouragement to acquire or use any substance, depictions of actual consumption

### Age-Appropriate Standards
All content appropriate for general adult audiences. Designs are provocative in the philosophical sense — they invite questions. They are not explicit, graphic in a disturbing way, or designed to exclude or demean any group.

### Community Standards
The brand's "you are ALL divine beings" philosophy is not aspirational — it's the policy. The community includes people of all backgrounds, all levels of familiarity with psychedelic culture, and all spiritual traditions. Gatekeeping, elitism, or mockery of outsiders is off-brand and off-limits.

### Legal Positioning
Product names and designs reference the cultural, philosophical, and experiential vocabulary of psychedelic culture as expressive art. They function as commentary, humor, and artistic expression — protected expression — and do not constitute instruction, promotion, or facilitation of illegal activity.
